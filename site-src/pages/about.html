About

Kevin M. Miller is an engineering leader, systems thinker, and diagnostic operator who works on problems where clarity matters more than theater. His default is evidence before certainty and precision before irreversible action.

That approach has been tested in software delivery, security, compliance, modernization, and documentation-heavy environments where systems fail slowly until they fail all at once. He is especially interested in how technical and organizational problems hide inside ambiguity, incentives, and legacy assumptions.

Today that work is expressed through Echelon Foundry, HelixNote, and Decision Posture: advisory for leaders navigating difficult technical change, evidence-grade documentation systems for high-stakes workflows, and public writing on judgment under uncertainty.

He has led engineering organizations, built diagnostic tooling, spoken publicly on software and systems, and helped teams think more clearly when the environment is constrained, political, or already under pressure.
